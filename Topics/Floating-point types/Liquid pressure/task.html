Liquid pressure

Write a program that reads the density of a liquid ρ and the height of a column h, and calculates the liquid pressure p.

  • We suppose the gravitational acceleration g is 9.8 m/s² (Earth).
  • Do not format the result.

Some explanations

When swimming underwater, water pressure is felt on the one's eardrums. The deeper you swim, the greater the pressure. The pressure felt is due to the weight of the water above. As one swims deeper, there is more water above them and, therefore, a greater pressure. The pressure a liquid exerts depends on its depth.

Liquid pressure also depends on the density of the liquid. If someone was submerged in a liquid more dense than water, the pressure would be correspondingly greater.
